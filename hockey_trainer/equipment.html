Equipment

All the equipment required to play underwater hockey

  

Swimsuit: Any will do, but polyester suits last the longest.

Flippers: Mares HPs are the brand of choice for beginners.

Fin Keepers: Used for securing your flippers to your ankle. Hard to find anywhere besides UWH vendors.

Glove: Generally made with a cotton glove covered in caulk for protection. Upgraded versions available fhrough UWH vendors.

Stick: Beginners should try wood first (it's least expensive and there's a good chance you will change your stick style). Also tryout other players' stick before you buy any if you can help it. Many different stick styles are available through UWH vendors.

Swim Cap/ Polo Cap/ Earguards: Either option keeps your hair out of the way, protects your ears from being kicked and lets the refs and the other team know if you are black or white. Option 1 is a polo cap, and option 2 is the swim cap with earguards attached to your mask.

Mask: Used for seeing underwater. Most players use the Aquasphere Sphera but any 2 lens, low profile mask will suffice.

Snorkel: Allows you to breath while on the surface and still see the plays happening below you.
